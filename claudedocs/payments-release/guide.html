What changed

From "status panel" to a real payments backbone

The old Payments tab was a Stripe Connect status screen with one tucked-away path (per-quote "Request Payment"). The new tab is a full workspace: invoices from scratch, quick pay links, real activity feed, monthly stats, and a transparent funds-flow diagram so users always know where the money is going.

New · Pay links

One-tap requests for a fixed amount

Pick an amount, optionally add a customer, share the link. Customer pays via Stripe Checkout. Best for deposits, ad-hoc work, tips.

New · Invoices

Itemized invoices with tax + due date

Build a real invoice (line items, qty, rate, tax, notes), get a branded /pay page that mirrors what your customer sees on Stripe Checkout. Optional invoice number per merchant.

Improved · Hub

Monthly stats + activity in one view

Paid this month, awaiting payment, net to your bank, Dyia fees collected. Filterable activity list with status + kind badges. Setup banner collapses when Stripe is fully live.

Improved · Transparency

"Where the money goes" explainer

4-step diagram showing customer pay → Dyia 0.75% fee → Stripe balance → auto-payout to bank. Removes confusion about who holds funds (we don't).

Money flow · Transparent for every payment

Where the money goes (Dyia never holds funds)

Stripe Connect Express routes funds straight from the customer to the merchant's bank. Dyia's 0.75% fee is deducted at charge time — we don't custody balances or process payouts ourselves. Merchants can open the Stripe dashboard from the app to see real-time payout schedules.

Talking point for the team

"Dyia never touches your money."

This is the single most important customer-facing line. Stripe Connect is the funds mover; Dyia is the workspace, the invoice builder, the activity feed, and the receipt ledger. The 0.75% is our piece of every successful charge — Stripe's processing fee is separate and not ours.
